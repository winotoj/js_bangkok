AUTHENTIC THAI CUISINE

Typically Thai

Thai food is incredibly flavourful. Here are some of the ingredients that are used to create these typically vibrant Thai flavours.

Main ingredients

CHILIES & SI RACHA

Fresh chillis and Si Racha sauce give Thai food its characteristic spyciness.

Photo Credits | CC0 1.0

FISH SAUCE

Fish Sauce, made from the brine in which anchovies ferment, is a key ingredient for many Thai dishes.

Photo Credits | CC BY 2.0

SHRIMP PASTE

Shrimp paste is a dried fish product and not only includes shrimp but also small fish, small prawn and sometimes krill.

Photo Credits | CC BY-SA 4.0

GALANGAL

Galangal is a cousin to ginger but tastes a bit different. It's more piney and sharp, with a strong citrus scent.

Photo Credits | CC BY 2.0

COCONUT

Coconut milk is a very popular food ingredient in Thai cuisine and gives sauces an especially rich taste.

Photo Credits | CC BY 2.0

PEANUT

As opposed to many Western cuisine that reserve peanuts for sweet deserts, peanut is regularly used in savory dishes and as a condiment in Thai cuisine.

Photo Credits | CC BY 2.0

More ingredients

Kaffir Lime
Kaffir lime is a citrus fruit. In Thai cuisine makes extensive use of the leaves and to a lesser extent the rind.
Lemongrass
Lemongrass is a plant of the grass family. Lemongrass is used for its scent, resembling that of lemons.
Thai Basil
Thai basil is a tye of basil with a flavour reminiscent of anise.
Garlic & Onion
Athough garlic and onion aren't used in some Asian cuisines, Thai food often uses these ingredients.
